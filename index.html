Gulab jamun

Ingredients:

To make Gulab Jamun, you will need 1 cup milk powder, ¼ cup all-purpose flour (maida), 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter), ¼ teaspoon baking soda, ¼ cup warm milk (adjust as needed), 1 teaspoon lemon juice (optional), and 1-2 tablespoons yogurt (optional) for the dough. For the sugar syrup, you will need 1.5 cups sugar, 1 cup water, 3-4 crushed cardamom pods, 1 teaspoon rose water (optional), and 1 teaspoon lemon juice (to prevent crystallization). Lastly, for frying, use oil or ghee.

Process:

To make Gulab Jamun, start by preparing a soft dough using khoya (mawa), all-purpose flour, baking soda, and cardamom powder, adding a little milk as needed. Shape the dough into small, smooth balls without cracks. Heat oil or ghee on low flame and deep-fry the balls until golden brown, stirring gently for even cooking. Meanwhile, prepare sugar syrup by boiling sugar, water, cardamom, and rose water until slightly sticky. Once the jamuns are fried, soak them in warm sugar syrup for at least 1-2 hours, allowing them to absorb the sweetness. Serve warm or chilled for a delicious, melt-in-the-mouth treat!

Burfi

Ingredients:

Barfi is made with condensed milk, sugar, ghee, and khoya or milk powder, flavored with cardamom and optionally saffron or rose water. It often includes chopped nuts like almonds, cashews, or pistachios for added texture and richness.

Process:

To make barfi, heat ghee in a pan, add condensed milk and khoya (or milk powder), and cook on low heat until thick. Stir in sugar, cardamom powder, and nuts, then mix well. Once the mixture leaves the sides of the pan, transfer it to a greased tray, flatten, and let it set. Cut into squares, garnish with nuts or saffron, and serve.
